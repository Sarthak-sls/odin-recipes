Meatloaf

"Description"

This article is about the meat dish. For the singer and actor, see Meat Loaf. American meatloaf with tomato ketchup Meatloaf is a dish of ground meat that has been combined with other ingredients and formed into the shape of a loaf, then baked or smoked. The final shape is either hand-formed on a baking tray, or pan-formed by cooking it in a loaf pan.[1] It is usually made with ground beef, although ground lamb, pork, veal, venison, poultry, and seafood are also used, sometimes in combination. Vegetarian adaptations of meatloaf may use imitation meat or pulses.

The cooked meatloaf can be sliced like a loaf of bread to make individual portions. It can easily become dry; therefore, various techniques exist to keep the dish moist, like mixing in bread crumbs and egg, covering it with sauce, wrapping it, or using moisture-enhancing ingredients in the mixture, such as filling it with fatty meats, rich cheeses, or vegetables.

The Austrian version of meatloaf is called faschierter Braten (literally "minced roast", from faschieren, "to mince", and ein Braten, "a roast"),[3] also known as falscher Hase, or 'fake rabbit'.[4] Most of the time, it is not filled (as it may be in Germany), though the variation im Speckmantel involves wrapping the exterior in ham or bacon before baking.[5] Often, it is served with mashed potatoes (when warm), or with Cumberland sauce (when cold).

Ingredients

  • Bread Crumbs
  • Eggs
  • Seasonings
  • Beef
  • Milk
  • Mushrooms

Steps

  1. Whisk together milk, bread crumbs, eggs, minced onion, salt, and sage.
  2. Stir in ground beef and mushrooms.
  3. Shape the mixture into a round loaf that will fit into your slow cooker.
  4. Place the formed loaf into your slow cooker and cook on low for five to six hours.
  5. Whisk together the ketchup, brown sugar, mustard, and Worcestershire sauce.
  6. Open the slow cooker and pour the glaze evenly over the loaf.
  7. Re-cover and continue to cook on low for about 15 more minutes.
  8. Remove the meatloaf and transfer to a serving plate.
  9. Cover with foil for about 10 minutes to trap in the juices, then slice and serve.
